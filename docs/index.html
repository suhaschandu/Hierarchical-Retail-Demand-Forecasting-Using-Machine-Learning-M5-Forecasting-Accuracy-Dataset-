01. The Challenge

Scalability Meets Hierarchy

Retail demand isn't just about total sales. It's a complex hierarchy: from the Item level to Departments, Categories, Stores, and States. Our task was to navigate the M5 dataset—one of the most challenging time-series competitions in history.

With 59 million rows of data, traditional time-series models like ARIMA or Prophet weren't enough. We needed a pipeline that was both massive and precise.

"The goal was not just to predict numbers, but to capture the rhythmic pulse of retail seasonality, price elasticities, and event-driven spikes."

02. Our Strategy

The Global-Local Blend

We implemented a unique Hybrid Architecture. Instead of relying on a single "god model," we combined two distinct approaches:

Global LightGBM

A massive model trained on the entire dataset to learn cross-store patterns and broad seasonality.

Local (Store x Cat)

Specialized models for each store and category to capture local market nuances and regional trends.

The 50/50 Blend: We found that averaging the predictions of these two models provided the most robust results, significantly reducing the WRMSSE from 0.71 to 0.627.

03. Feature Engineering

The 48 Signals

A model is only as good as its features. We engineered 48 unique signals across four categories:

Temporal

Wday, month, year, event_types, and snap_days (SNAP benefits days).

Lags & Rolling

7, 14, 28, and 30-day sales lags and rolling means to capture momentum.

Price Dynamics

Price change from last week, relative price to department average.
